Sunday batch — ~90 min total
One Sunday session. Monday to Saturday is reheat + assemble. Exact steps, timings, and what to look for so nothing burns or stays raw.
1Slow cooker butter chicken (Vasu's lunch)20 min + 6 hr passive
Ingredients — 7 serves
2.2 kg raw chicken breast (whole, no cutting)  ·  2 large red onions finely sliced  ·  2 × 400g cans diced tomato  ·  3 tbsp butter (1 for pan, 2 for sauce)  ·  1 tbsp neutral oil  ·  2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste  ·  150 g Greek yogurt (added at very end)  ·  Spices: 2 tsp garam masala · 1 tsp kashmiri chilli · 1 tsp cumin powder · 1 tsp coriander powder · ½ tsp turmeric · 1.5 tsp salt
1
Heat 1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter in a wide pan on medium-high. Add sliced onions. Cook 10–12 min stirring every 2 min until deep golden-brown and starting to catch at edges. Don't rush — the colour is the flavour.
2
Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir constantly 90 seconds until the raw smell turns toasty. Splash of water if it sticks.
3
Add both tomato cans + all spices + salt. Cook 10–12 min on medium-high, stirring every 2 min. Mixture thickens down — oil separates at the pan edges, colour deepens to brick red. Needs to be thick, not watery.
4
Add remaining 2 tbsp butter. Stir until melted. Pour entire base into slow cooker.
5
Place whole raw chicken breasts on top, push down until mostly submerged. No need to brown first.
6
Set to LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3.5 hours. Lid on. Do not open during cooking.
7
After cooking: chicken falls apart when pressed with spoon. Use two forks to shred directly in the pot — pull apart into strips, stir into sauce. Should look and taste like proper butter chicken.
8
Turn to LOW or off. Add 150 g Greek yogurt one spoon at a time, stirring gently. Do not boil after yogurt — it splits. Taste and adjust salt. Cool 15 min lid off.
9
Portion into 7 equal containers — one large ladle each. Fridge 5 days, freeze remaining 2.
Too thin: lid off, HIGH for 30 min more. Too thick: splash of hot water, stir. Oily on top: normal, stir when reheating. 7 serves · ~80g P · ~682 cal per serve (before rice)
2Keema dal — pressure cooker (everyone)40 min active
Ingredients — ~14 serves
1.5 kg raw chicken breast, cut into 5 cm chunks  ·  700 g raw red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed  ·  3 large onions finely chopped  ·  2 tbsp oil  ·  2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste  ·  3 tomatoes chopped or 1×400g can  ·  2 L water  ·  Spices: 2 tsp cumin seeds · 1.5 tsp turmeric · 2 tsp garam masala · 1.5 tsp coriander powder · 1 tsp chilli powder · 2 tsp salt  ·  Tadka: 1 tbsp ghee · 1 tsp cumin seeds · 2 dried red chillies · 4 garlic cloves sliced
1
Open pressure cooker, no lid. Medium-high heat. Add oil. Once hot, add cumin seeds — sizzle immediately. Wait 20 sec until they darken.
2
Add onions. Cook 10–12 min stirring regularly until deep golden, almost jammy. They shrink to half their volume. Add tiny splash of water if catching.
3
Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir constantly 2 min until raw smell disappears.
4
Add tomatoes + all spices + salt. Cook 8–10 min on medium-high, stirring every 2 min. Tomatoes break down completely. Oil separates at the edges — this is the sign it's ready. Colour: dark reddish-brown.
5
Add chicken chunks. Stir to coat. Cook 4–5 min on high until outside of chicken turns white/opaque. Inside doesn't need to be done — pressure will finish it.
6
Add rinsed lentils. Stir 30 sec. Add 2 L cold water. Scrape the bottom to release any stuck masala — burnt base will ruin the whole batch.
7
Close lid, put weight on. High heat until 4 whistles, then immediately lowest flame for 12 min, then off. Do not open until pressure fully releases — 15–20 min. You'll hear the weight drop.
8
Open carefully (face away). Dal should be thick and creamy. Stir — chicken falls apart into strands. No identifiable pieces should remain. Too thick: add hot water. Too thin: simmer open 5 min.
9
Tadka: Small pan, heat ghee. Add cumin seeds (they'll splutter), then garlic slices and dried chillies. Cook 45–60 sec until garlic is golden not brown. Pour over dal, stir immediately.
10
Taste, adjust salt. Portion into containers: ~200 g cooked per serve for partner lunch, ~200 g for Vasu dinner on alternating days. Fridge 5 days, freeze the rest.
If you smell burning at step 4 or 6: water immediately, scrape the bottom before continuing. ~14 serves · ~49g P · ~438 cal · chicken completely undetectable

4Basmati rice batch20 min
1
Rinse 700 g raw basmati until water runs clear (4–5 rinses). Soak 10 min if possible.
2
Add 1,050 mL water + 1 tsp salt + 1 tsp oil. Bring to boil. Then lowest flame, lid on, 13 min. Do not lift lid.
3
Turn off. Lid stays on 5 more min. Fluff gently with fork.
4
Portion warm: Vasu ~150 g cooked per bag (from 55 g raw). Partner ~100 g cooked per bag (from 37 g raw). Freeze half — 90 sec microwave from frozen.

5Masala base — freeze in cubes15 min
1
Finely chop 4 onions + 5 tomatoes. Heat 2 tbsp oil in wide pan on high.
2
Cook onions 10 min until dark golden. Add 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, cook 2 min. Add tomatoes + 1 tsp salt + 1 tsp turmeric. Cook 10 min until it pulls from the sides as one thick mass.
3
Cool. Spoon into ice cube tray (~1 tbsp per slot). Freeze solid, transfer to zip-lock bag. Drop 2–3 cubes into anything during the week — eggs, dal, tuna — instant depth, zero effort.
~20 cubes · freezes 4 weeks · use everywhere
