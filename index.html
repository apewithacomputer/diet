Sunday batch — ~80 min, zero cooking all week
Everything below is a one-time Sunday job. Monday to Saturday is reheating and assembly only. Both of you work — this is the only way it actually sticks.
1Slow cooker butter chicken — Vasu5 min + 6 hrs
Take 2.2 kg raw chicken breast. No cutting needed — whole breasts go in. Add to slow cooker: 700 g tomato passata, 2 tbsp butter, 3 heaped tsp garam masala, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp kashmiri chilli (for colour and mild heat), 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, salt, 100 g yogurt. Stir, set on LOW for 6 hrs or HIGH for 3.5 hrs. At the end, use two forks to shred the chicken directly in the pot — it should fall apart. Stir and taste. This becomes a thick, rich, saucy butter chicken. Portion into 7 containers (~300 g cooked per serve for Vasu). Fridge 5 days, freeze the rest.
7 lunch serves for Vasu · genuinely tastes like the real thing
2Keema dal — Partner30 min
Take 1.1 kg raw chicken breast, mince it (blender, or ask butcher). In a heavy pot: heat oil, add cumin seeds, then onion — cook until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomato, all the spices (turmeric, garam masala, coriander, chilli, salt). Cook masala 5 min until thick and dark. Add the raw mince — break it up and cook until it turns white and then starts browning, about 8 min. Add 500 g raw red lentils (pre-rinsed) + 1.5 L water. Simmer 20 min. The lentils absorb everything. The mince completely disappears into the dal — no pieces, no texture, just a thick, rich, spiced dal. Portion into 7 serves (~160 g cooked dal + mince). Fridge 5 days, freeze the rest.
7 lunch serves for partner · chicken is completely undetectable
3Dinner dal — both20 min
Separately, rinse 700 g raw red lentils and pressure cook 8 min (or simmer 20 min). This is the plain base dal used in dinners all week — add flavour when reheating by throwing in a masala cube (see step 5). Keeps 5 days. Both pull from this for dinner.
~14 dinner serves combined
4Hard-boiled eggs — 1812 min
Boil 18 eggs for exactly 10 min. Ice bath immediately. Leave unpeeled in a bowl in the fridge — they last 7 days unpeeled. Both people pull from this all week for breakfast and dinner. Make a second batch of 18 on Wednesday or Thursday.
36 total needed across the week for both
5Masala base — freeze in cubes15 min
Fry 4 large onions + 5 tomatoes + 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste in 2 tbsp oil on high heat until it becomes a very thick, dark paste. Cool completely. Pour into an ice cube tray (~1 tbsp per slot). Freeze solid, transfer to a zip-lock bag. During the week, drop 2–3 cubes into any pan — eggs, dal, tuna — instant restaurant flavour in 60 seconds. This is what stops food from feeling sad and dry.
~20 cubes · freezes 4 weeks · use everywhere
6Rice batch20 min
Cook 700 g raw basmati — rinse until water runs clear, then 1:1.5 water ratio, lid on, 15 min. Yields ~1.9 kg cooked. Portion into bags: Vasu gets 55 g raw equivalent (~150 g cooked) per bag, partner gets 37 g raw equivalent (~100 g cooked). Freeze half immediately. Reheat in 90 seconds.
~18 combined serves · the foundation of every lunch
