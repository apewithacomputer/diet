1Slow cooker butter chicken (Vasu's lunch)20 min + 6 hr passive
Ingredients — 7 serves
2.2 kg raw chicken breast (whole, no cutting)  ·  2 large red onions finely sliced  ·  2 × 400g cans diced tomato  ·  3 tbsp butter (1 for pan, 2 for sauce)  ·  1 tbsp oil  ·  2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste  ·  150g Greek yogurt (added at very end)  ·  Spices: 2 tsp garam masala · 1 tsp kashmiri chilli · 1 tsp cumin powder · 1 tsp coriander powder · ½ tsp turmeric · 1.5 tsp salt
1
Heat 1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter in a wide pan on medium-high. Add sliced onions. Cook 10–12 min stirring every 2 min until deep golden-brown and starting to catch at the edges. Don't rush — the colour is flavour.
2
Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir constantly 90 seconds until the raw pungent smell turns toasty. Add a splash of water if it sticks.
3
Add both tomato cans + all spices + salt. Cook 10–12 min on medium-high, stirring every 2 min. The mixture should thicken right down and oil will separate around the edges of the pan. Colour deepens from orange to brick red. This needs to be thick — not watery.
4
Add remaining 2 tbsp butter. Stir in until melted. Turn off heat. Pour the entire base into the slow cooker.
5
Place whole raw chicken breasts on top of the base. Push them down so mostly submerged. Do not brown the chicken first — the slow cook handles it.
6
Set slow cooker to LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3.5 hours. Lid on. Walk away — do not lift the lid during cooking.
7
After cooking: chicken should fall apart when pressed with a spoon. Use two forks to shred directly in the pot — pull breasts apart into chunks and strips, stir into the sauce. It looks like proper butter chicken at this point.
8
Turn to LOW or off. Add 150g Greek yogurt one spoon at a time, stirring gently. Do not boil after adding yogurt — it will split and curdle. Taste, adjust salt. Let it cool 15 min with lid off.
9
Portion into 7 equal containers — one large ladle per serve. Fridge 5 days, freeze the remaining 2.
Too thin: leave lid off final 30 min on HIGH to reduce. Too thick: add a splash of hot water and stir. If sauce looks oily on top: totally normal, stir when reheating. 7 serves · ~80g P · ~682 cal each (before rice)
2Keema dal — pressure cooker (everyone's dinner + partner's lunch)40 min active
Ingredients — ~14 serves
1.5 kg raw chicken breast, cut into large chunks (~5 cm)  ·  700g raw red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed until water runs clear  ·  3 large red onions finely chopped  ·  2 tbsp neutral oil  ·  2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste  ·  3 medium tomatoes chopped (or 1 × 400g can)  ·  2 L water  ·  Spices: 2 tsp cumin seeds · 1.5 tsp turmeric · 2 tsp garam masala · 1.5 tsp coriander powder · 1 tsp chilli powder · 2 tsp salt  ·  Tadka: 1 tbsp ghee · 1 tsp cumin seeds · 2 dried red chillies · 4 garlic cloves sliced
1
Open pressure cooker, no lid. Heat on medium-high. Add oil. Once shimmering, add cumin seeds — should sizzle immediately. Wait 20 sec until they darken slightly.
2
Add onions. Cook 10–12 min stirring regularly. Target: deep golden brown, shrunk to about half their original volume. They should look almost jammy. Add tiny splash of water if catching.
3
Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir constantly 2 min. Raw pungent smell fades and becomes toasty. If sticking, splash of water and scrape bottom.
4
Add tomatoes + all spices + salt. Cook 8–10 min on medium-high, stirring every 2 min. Tomatoes should completely break down. Oil separates at edges of the masala — this is the sign it's ready. Colour: dark reddish-brown.
5
Add chicken breast chunks. Stir to coat. Cook 4–5 min on high stirring occasionally. Outside of chicken turns white/opaque. Inside doesn't need to be cooked through — pressure cooker finishes it.
6
Add rinsed lentils. Stir everything together 30 sec. Add 2 L cold water. Stir. Scrape the bottom — any stuck masala will burn under pressure.
7
Close lid and put weight on. High heat until 4 whistles, then immediately turn to lowest flame for 12 min, then turn off. Do not open until pressure fully releases on its own — about 15–20 min. You'll hear the weight drop or see steam stop.
8
Open carefully (away from face). Dal should be thick and creamy. Stir — the chicken chunks will fall apart into strands. Use a spoon to break any remaining pieces. No identifiable chicken should remain — just a rich, thick dal. Too thick: add hot water and stir. Too thin: simmer open 5 min.
9
Tadka: Small pan, heat ghee until shimmering. Add cumin seeds (they splutter), then sliced garlic and dried chillies. Cook 45–60 sec until garlic is golden (not dark brown). Pour over dal and stir in immediately.
10
Taste and adjust salt. Portion: partner lunch ~200g each (7 serves), Vasu dinner ~200g each (alternating days). Fridge 5 days, freeze the rest.
If anything smells like it's burning at step 4 or 6: add water immediately and scrape the bottom before continuing. Burnt masala makes the whole batch bitter. ~14 serves · ~49g P · ~438 cal · chicken undetectable

1

2

5Masala base — freeze in cubes15 min
1
Finely chop 4 large onions + 5 tomatoes. Heat 2 tbsp oil in wide pan on high.
2
Cook onions 10 min until dark golden. Add 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, cook 2 min. Add tomatoes + 1 tsp salt + 1 tsp turmeric. Cook 10 min on high until very thick — it pulls away from the sides as one mass.
3
Cool completely. Spoon into ice cube tray (~1 tbsp per slot). Freeze solid, transfer to a zip-lock bag. Drop 2–3 cubes into any pan during the week — eggs, dal, tuna. Instant depth.
~20 cubes · lasts 4 weeks frozen · the secret weapon
