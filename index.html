Sunday batch — ~90 min total, zero cooking all week
Do everything below on Sunday. Monday to Saturday is microwave + assemble only. Both recipes below have exact steps, timings and what to look for so nothing burns or stays raw.
1 Butter chicken — slow cooker (Vasu's lunch) 20 min + 6 hrs passive
Ingredients (7 serves)
2.2 kg raw chicken breast (whole, no cutting needed)  ·  2 large red onions finely sliced  ·  2 × 400g cans diced tomato  ·  3 tbsp butter (split: 1 for base, 2 in sauce)  ·  1 tbsp neutral oil  ·  2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste  ·  150g Greek yogurt (added at end)  ·  Spices: 2 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp kashmiri chilli, 1 tsp cumin powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp turmeric, salt
1Heat 1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter in a wide pan on medium-high. Add sliced onions. Cook 10–12 min stirring every 2 min until they're golden-brown and starting to catch slightly at the edges. Don't rush this — the colour is flavour.
2Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir constantly for 90 seconds until the raw smell changes to something toasty. If it starts sticking, splash a tbsp of water.
3Add both cans of tomato + all the spices + 1 tsp salt. Cook on medium-high for 10–12 min stirring every couple of minutes. You want the mixture to thicken right down and the oil to start separating at the edges of the pan. The colour should deepen from orange to a darker brick red. This is the base — it needs to be thick and cooked down, not watery.
4Add remaining 2 tbsp butter, stir in. Turn off heat. Pour the entire base into the slow cooker.
5Place whole raw chicken breasts directly on top of the base in the slow cooker. Push them down so they're mostly submerged. No need to brown the chicken separately.
6Set to LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3.5 hours. Walk away. Don't lift the lid.
7After cooking: the chicken should fall apart when you push it with a spoon. Use two forks to shred it directly in the pot — pull the breasts apart into chunks and strips, mix into the sauce. It will look like proper butter chicken at this point.
8Turn slow cooker to LOW (or off if it's off heat). Stir in 150g Greek yogurt one spoon at a time. Do not boil after adding yogurt or it will split. Taste, adjust salt.
9Portion into 7 equal containers. Each serve ≈ one large ladle. Fridge for 5 days, freeze the remaining 2.
If the sauce looks too thin: leave the lid off for the last 30 min on HIGH to reduce. If it looks too thick: add a splash of water and stir. 7 lunch serves · 76g P · 484 cal per serve (before rice)
2 Keema dal — pressure cooker (everyone's dinner + partner's lunch) 40 min active
Ingredients (~14 serves)
1.5 kg raw chicken breast, cut into large chunks (~5 cm pieces)  ·  700g raw red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed until water runs clear  ·  3 large red onions finely chopped  ·  2 tbsp neutral oil  ·  2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste  ·  3 medium tomatoes chopped, or 1 × 400g can diced tomato  ·  2 L water  ·  Spices: 2 tsp cumin seeds, 1.5 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp garam masala, 1.5 tsp coriander powder, 1 tsp chilli powder, 2 tsp salt  ·  Tadka finish: 1 tbsp ghee, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 2 dried red chillies, 4 garlic cloves sliced
1Heat the pressure cooker on medium-high without the lid. Add oil. Once hot, add cumin seeds — they should sizzle immediately. Wait 20 seconds until they darken slightly.
2Add chopped onions. Cook 10–12 min stirring regularly until deep golden brown. They will shrink down to about half. Push to the sides if they start catching — add a splash of water if needed. Dark golden (not burnt) is the target.
3Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir constantly for 2 min. The raw pungent smell should fade and become toasty.
4Add tomatoes + all spices + salt. Cook on medium-high for 8–10 min stirring frequently. The tomatoes should completely break down and the oil will separate from the masala around the edges. If it sticks, add a tiny splash of water and scrape the bottom.
5Add the chicken breast chunks. Stir to coat in the masala. Cook 4–5 min on high stirring occasionally — the outside of the chicken should turn white/opaque. It doesn't need to be cooked through yet — the pressure cooker will finish it.
6Add rinsed lentils, stir everything together for 30 seconds. Add 2 L cold water. Stir. Check nothing is stuck to the bottom — scrape if needed.
7Close the pressure cooker lid and weight. Cook on high heat until you get 4 whistles, then immediately reduce to low heat for 12 min, then turn off completely. Do not open until the pressure fully releases on its own — this takes 15–20 min. You will hear/see the weight drop.
8Open carefully. The dal should be thick and creamy. The chicken chunks will have completely broken down — use a spoon to stir and the remaining pieces will shred into strands through the dal. You should not be able to identify individual chicken pieces. If dal is too thick: add hot water and stir. If too thin: simmer open for 5 min.
9Tadka finish: Heat ghee in a small pan until shimmering. Add cumin seeds (they'll splutter), then sliced garlic and dried chillies. Cook 45–60 seconds until garlic is golden (not dark brown). Pour over the dal immediately and stir in.
10Taste and adjust salt. Portion: partner lunch serves ~200g each (7 serves), dinner serves ~160g each, Vasu dinner serves ~200g each (7 serves). Fridge 5 days, freeze the rest.
If you smell anything burning at step 4 or 6: add water immediately and scrape the bottom before continuing. Burnt masala will make the whole batch bitter. ~14 serves · 49g P · 438 cal per serve · chicken fully dissolved into dal
3 Hard-boiled eggs — 18 at once 12 min
1Place 18 eggs in a large pot. Cover with cold water by 2 cm.
2Bring to a boil on high. Once boiling, reduce to medium and cook exactly 10 min.
3Drain, fill pot with cold water + ice (or just cold tap water). Leave 5 min. Eggs stop cooking and peel easily.
4Leave unpeeled in a bowl or container in the fridge. They last 7 days unpeeled. Both pull from the same bowl all week.
Make a second batch of 18 on Wednesday evening
4 Basmati rice batch 20 min
1Rinse 700g raw basmati in cold water 4–5 times until water runs mostly clear. Soak 10 min (optional, makes it fluffier).
2Add to pot with 1050 mL water (1:1.5 ratio), 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp oil. Bring to boil, then lowest heat, lid on tight for 13 min. Do not open.
3Turn off. Leave lid on for 5 min to steam. Fluff with a fork.
4Portion immediately while warm: Vasu gets ~150g cooked per bag (from 55g raw), partner gets ~100g cooked per bag (from 37g raw). Freeze half. Microwave 90 sec from frozen.
~18 serves combined · freezes perfectly
5 Masala base — freeze in cubes (optional but game-changing) 15 min
1Finely chop 4 large onions + 5 tomatoes. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan on high.
2Cook onions 10 min until dark golden. Add 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, cook 2 min. Add tomatoes + 1 tsp salt + 1 tsp turmeric. Cook 10 min on high until very thick — it should pull away from the sides of the pan as one mass.
3Cool completely. Spoon into ice cube tray (~1 heaped tbsp each). Freeze solid (2 hrs), then transfer to a zip-lock bag.
Drop 2–3 cubes into any pan with eggs, dal, or tuna. Instant restaurant flavour, zero daily effort. This is the difference between food that feels like punishment and food that actually tastes good. ~20 cubes · lasts 4 weeks frozen
